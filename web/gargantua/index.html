Gargantua

A simple visual sketch of a spinning black hole. The purple disc is hot material orbiting the hole; gravity bends the far side into arcs above and below the dark center as the view yaws side to side.

Warped light The cyan-white arcs are light from behind the black hole, bent around toward us.
Accretion disc Purple gas races around the black hole and appears brighter on one side.
Event horizon The black center is the point where light can no longer escape.
Lensed image Gravity makes a second, distorted view of the same disc.
Purple and magenta: hot orbiting gas in the accretion disc.
Cyan-white: light bent by gravity around the black hole.
Black center: the event horizon, where escape becomes impossible.
Gold-white rim: the photon ring, a bright edge made by extreme lensing.
Tiny astronaut: a scale marker floating near the warped disc.
